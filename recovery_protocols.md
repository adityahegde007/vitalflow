# Post-Surgical Recovery Protocols

## General Recovery Rules (All Procedures)
- Confirm patient identity before advice (Patient ID 100-110 in demo mode).
- Encourage hydration, nutrition, sleep, and medication adherence.
- Keep incision clean and dry unless surgeon instructions say otherwise.
- Use pain scale tracking (0-10) at least twice daily.
- Advise immediate escalation for emergency symptoms.

## Symptom Triage
### Normal
- Mild pain improving day to day.
- Mild swelling or bruising near incision.
- Fatigue in first 3-7 days.
- Low-grade temperature under 38.0 C in first 24-48 hours.

### Concerning (Needs Same-Day Clinical Review)
- Fever 38.0 C to 38.5 C lasting over 24 hours.
- Redness extending beyond incision edges.
- Increasing pain after initial improvement.
- New drainage, foul odor, or persistent vomiting.
- Limited mobility worse than expected recovery trend.

### Critical (Emergency)
- Chest pain.
- Shortness of breath.
- Heavy bleeding.
- Confusion, fainting, or severe allergic reaction.
- Signs of sepsis (high fever, chills, rapid heart rate, severe weakness).

## Procedure-Specific Guidance
### Appendectomy
- Expected discomfort: lower abdomen tenderness for 3-5 days.
- Monitor for: increasing right lower quadrant pain, persistent fever, vomiting.
- Activity: short walks day 1 onward, avoid heavy lifting for 2-4 weeks.

### Knee Replacement
- Expected discomfort: swelling and stiffness for several weeks.
- Monitor for: calf pain/swelling (possible DVT), inability to bear weight, worsening joint redness.
- Activity: follow physiotherapy plan; elevate and ice as directed.

### Gallbladder Removal
- Expected discomfort: mild shoulder or abdominal discomfort after laparoscopy.
- Monitor for: jaundice, persistent nausea/vomiting, severe upper abdominal pain.
- Diet: small low-fat meals initially; advance as tolerated.

## Follow-Up Scheduling Rules
- Normal trajectory: next routine follow-up in 3-7 days.
- Concerning symptoms: schedule next available same-day/next-day slot.
- Repeated concerning logs in 48 hours: escalate to nurse/clinician alert.
- Critical symptoms: do not schedule routine slot first; trigger emergency instruction immediately.

## Documentation Rules
- Log every triage decision with:
  - patient_id
  - symptom summary
  - severity (NORMAL, CONCERNING, CRITICAL)
  - action taken
  - timestamp
- When appointment is booked, log booked slot and rationale.
